Understanding Navigation Limits

Coastal cruising insurance defines specific cruising areas where your vessel is covered. These limits are typically measured in nautical miles from designated ports or coastlines.

Typical Cruising Zones

  • Within 50-100 nautical miles of home port
  • Defined regional cruising areas (Gulf, Sounds, etc.)
  • Specific anchorages and harbors

Exceeding Limits

If you plan occasional offshore passages or extended cruising beyond your defined area:

  • Notify your insurer before departing
  • Purchase extended coverage for longer trips
  • Consider blue water coverage if cruising offshore regularly

Coastal Cruising Areas

North Island Coastal

Coverage for cruising in the Bay of Islands, Hauraki Gulf, Bay of Plenty, and Tasman coastal waters. These routes are ideal for year-round cruising with generally protected anchorages and established cruising communities.

Includes: Auckland, Whangarei, Tauranga regions

South Island Coastal

Coverage for the Marlborough Sounds, Fiordland, and Southland coastal cruising. These regions offer dramatic scenery and can involve longer passages between anchorages.

Includes: Nelson, Southland, West Coast regions

Strait Cruising

Special coverage for Cook Strait and Tasman Strait passages. These routes connect North and South Island cruising grounds with reliable weather windows.

Seasonal cruising March-October recommended
